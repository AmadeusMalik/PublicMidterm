Kenneth Bruce Gorelick, better known as “Kenny G”. Born June 5, 1956. He was in Seattle, Washington, and started playing the saxophone at only 10 years old. Before graduating from Ubiversity of Washington, he was a sideman for Barry White's Love Unlimited Orchestra while still in highschool. I was introduced to his music at a very young age from my parents. What made me want to choose him, is when I think of jazz, I think of a smooth saxophone, which is Kennys signature. I love the versatility that comes with a saxophone. Kenny just does something special when it comes to setting the tone with it. The first time I heard him I was maybe about 10 years old, through the quiet storm radio station. Kenny influenced many modern musicians like Kanye West and Usher.

John Coltrane was one of Kennys influences in college. He also word with other famed musicians alike, such as Duke Elligton and Miles Davis. Some of his early influences were Count Basie And Lester Young. Besides the fact that they both play the saxophone, the smoothness in Kennys and Coltrane’s slower, softer songs. One song I’ve had in my personal playlist by John is ‘Lonnie’s Lament’. So, I was a little familiar with Coltrane before I knew he had major influence on Kenny.

His second influence, Walter Theodore Rollins, better known as Sonny Rollins. Born on September 7th, 1930. Along with Coltrane, Kenny considers him a ‘jazz master’. I can find similarities in some songs when they use the saxophone to ‘talk’ even though there’s no lyrics. At 93 years old , Sonny sold his music catalog for 2 billion in the beginning of 2023.

Another influence on Kenny was Stan Getz, full name; Stanley Gayetski. Born February 2nd, 1927, and died June 6th, 1991. Getz played a lot slower and played a bit more emotionally. Very close to Kennys songs now, that have a lot of emotion and don’t require lyrics to really convey how they’re feeling.
